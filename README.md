# quantum-project-proposal

# Open-Source Modeling of Photoluminescence Dynamics in Semiconductor Quantum Dots

## 📌 Overview

This project develops an open-source Python toolkit for simulating photoluminescence (PL) spectra and exciton dynamics in semiconductor quantum dots (QDs).

Quantum dots are nanoscale semiconductor systems with discrete energy levels due to quantum confinement effects. They are key components in quantum photonics and are widely studied as potential single-photon sources and quantum bits (qubits).

This project bridges semiconductor physics and open quantum system simulations using Python-based scientific computing tools.

## 🎯 Objectives

- Implement quantum confinement models (Brus and Kayanuma equations)
- Simulate exciton population dynamics using Lindblad master equations (QuTiP)
- Generate photoluminescence spectra with realistic broadening effects
- Study effects of size, temperature, and environment on optical emission
- Develop educational Jupyter notebooks for quantum dot physics

------------------------------------------------------------

## 🧠 Physics Background

### Quantum Confinement

Energy levels in quantum dots depend strongly on particle size, leading to tunable band gaps.

### Open Quantum Systems

Exciton dynamics are modeled using density matrices and Lindblad evolution involving:
- Decoherence
- Radiative decay
- Environmental interactions
